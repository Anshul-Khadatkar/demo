Tribute to Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, is an Indian cricketer and former captain of the Indian national team. Known for his aggressive batting style and exceptional run-scoring ability, Kohli is considered one of the best batsmen in the world. He has numerous records to his name, including the fastest player to score 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs).

Kohli has received numerous accolades and awards throughout his career, including the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year on multiple occasions. He has been a crucial part of the Indian team's success in various formats of the game, leading them to victories in major tournaments. Kohli's passion, dedication, and leadership qualities have made him an inspiration to aspiring cricketers around the world.

Off the field, Kohli is known for his philanthropic work and actively supports various charitable causes. He is also involved in several brand endorsements and has become a popular figure both within and outside the cricketing world.

Runs

Virat Kohli has scored over 12,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs) with an average of 59.07.

He has also scored more than 7,500 runs in Test matches with an average of 52.04.

Centuries

Kohli has an impressive record of 43 centuries in ODIs and 27 centuries in Test matches.

Achievements

Virat Kohli has received several prestigious awards, including the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year in 2017 and 2018.

He has also been honored with the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, India's highest sporting award, in 2018.
